Mango Shortcakes

8 1 h 20 min

Ingredients

Fruit

Quantity Name
2 Mango
125 g Blueberries
150 g Strawberries
1 tbsp Honey
1.5 tsp Lime

Cream

Quantity Name
0.5 tsp Gelatin
1 tbsp Water
250 ml Heavy cream
2 tbsp Icing sugar
0.5 tsp Coconut extract

Shortcakes

Quantity Name
350 g Flour
60 g Sugar
7 g Dry yeast
1 tbsp Lime zest
1.5 tsp Salt
125 g Vegetable shortening
1 Egg
250 ml Buttermilk

Step 1

Preheat an oven to 200Â°C. Grease a baking sheet.

Step 2

Combine mango, blueberries and strawberries in a large bowl. Drizzle with lime juice and honey. Mix well. Cover. Reserve in refrigerator.

Step 3

Beat cream in the large bowl with an electric mixer. When cream begins to thicken, sprinkle in icing sugar and coconut extract. Mix in gelatin all at once. Whip until soft peaks form. Cover and refrigerate.

Step 4

Whisk flour, dry yeast, sugar, lime zest and salt in a large bowl. Cut in the shortening with a knife or pastry blender until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Beat the egg and buttermilk in a small bowl, lightly stir into flour mixture just until moistened.

Step 5

Turn out onto a floured work surface. Knead 3 to 4 times, or until dough just comes together. Roll to 1.2cm thickness. Cut with round biscuit cutter. Place on prepared baking sheet.

Step 6

Bake shortcakes in preheated oven until golden brown, about 15 to 18 minutes. Allow to cool slightly. Split each cake in half horizontally.

Step 7

To assemble, arrange fruit on the bottom half of each cake. Spoon or pipe cream evenly over fruit. Top each with remaining biscuit halves.
